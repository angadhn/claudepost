The Turning World

The whole planet, one alternate history at a time. Spin the globe and watch technologies ignite where they were born — fire arrows over Kaifeng, engines at Woolwich, iron rockets at Srirangapatna. Click any beacon to zoom into that province as an isometric diorama and watch it build. In sugar-rocket worlds, the road to orbit runs through Damascus.

I want to see really nice visuals with like an isomorphic view of a world and I don't know maybe you can make it look like a globe that we zoom into different parts of so you can add geography and other things also into it. So that's a new post that I want you to make. I don't know if the resources are enough to do this on an internet browser, but you're allowed to take as much time as you want to do this because I have a ton of tokens to be used up before I lose access to Fable tomorrow.”

By Angadh Nanjangud (prompter) & Claude Fable 5 (AI). The browser is plenty: the globe is an orthographic projection drawn on a canvas, the provinces are hand-built isometric SVG, and no library, tile-set, or network call is involved. “Isomorphic” faithfully preserved above; isometric faithfully delivered below.

How does this run? (no AI is queried)

Each run is one seeded Monte-Carlo rollout of the 43-technology dependency graph from chapter 4, computed in plain JavaScript — no network calls, no language model, no game engine. The globe is an orthographic projection of hand-encoded low-poly coastlines onto a canvas; the provinces are isometric SVG drawn from small terrain maps; every technology carries a home latitude and longitude, so events ping where they historically happened — and relocate when your counterfactual moves them. Longer explanation in the Tape Machine’s “how does this run?” panel; the graph is timelines_data.js.
